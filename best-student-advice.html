I recommend taking a deep surface approach to the lessons during this program.

You can find the free study path on this webpage. Learn how to Program

The Program study path is broken up in the following way:

Homework -> Morning Warmup -> Creative Implementation.

Here’s what you should do:

  • Follow along with the homework. Actually do it that night, just follow along with the video, don’t think much about it. Do the homework!
  • Follow the instructions the next day. You will likely start with implementing the homework from the night before. Just go along with the lesson as it’s printed, this is the warm up, don’t skip it.
  • Now you can get creative!. After the homework implementation, you’re given an assignment that implements the concepts you just studied in a new form. This is where you really learn. You can do it how you want (but you will likely really want the information you just learned).

Finally, don’t get lost in front-end minutia, unless that’s the lesson or you’re done with the day’s assignment. This is the design rabbit hole. It’s deep and you’re new, take it easy.

When you’re about halfway done with your studies at this program, take some steps to make your job search life easier.

  1. Make a Portfolio. Mine is hosted on GitHub Pages for free, using Jekyll, with a template. You do not need a hand-built site if you’re not a front-end developer, just get one up that looks good and be done with it.
  2. Get your LinkedIn in order. Follow the guide on this program in this regard, it paid off for me.

  3. Get your Resume put together. One page. No references to jobs that have nothing to do with coding. One page. Write it like some great code, short and effective. One page.
  4. Get a decent Cover letter together. You do not need a custom cover letter for every job, you just need some parts to be custom. I have background information that doesn’t change in my cover letters, you can do the same thing.
  5. The above will take you very far in the general arena of job hunting. Angelist also allows for sending notes and if you go there, I recommend that you send them. Treat these like micro-cover letters. Get a general template that you can alter some spots and hammer those out. Make sure your Angelist profile is complete though, just like your LinkedIn.
